Description (for Tony Conrad)

See score >

“Can a listener feel that they have any dignity when the values of music are registered against “ideals”? After all, the work and the joy of listening are in the internal fraction between what is and what is imagined. And there ain’t no respect in a prefigured imaginary.”
-Tony Conrad, “Slapping Pythagoras”

Sound is a multivalent, protean disturbance, which only our senses and imagination massage into coherent forms. In this poetry disguised as music, an audience is met with the richness of two voices, two streams of language, triangulated by some distant well beyond the misty horizon.

This is a model for a more sociable music, incomplete without the imaginative work of the audience, parsing between two dispatches from utopia.
